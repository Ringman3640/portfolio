Why Computer Science?

I fell in love with the practice of programming when I started playing LittleBigPlanet 2 as a child. The LittleBigPlanet game series allows players to create their own levels and has its own logic system similar to block programming. I was facinated by the ability to develop complex abilities and behaviors from scratch and addicted to watching the whole system slowly come together into a complex, interwoven machine.

I eventually got into language-based programming in my freshman year of high school in an introductory computer science class. I learned how to write very simple HTML and CSS websites, along with a small amount of Javascript. It was the most fun I had in a high school course, abeit helped by my friends' company.

I wouldn't delve too deeply into developing my programming skills until I began Running Start. Everett Community College provided multiple introductory computer science courses that I knew I wanted to join from my past experiences. Since then, I have drastically improved my programming skills and knowledge.

Computer programming, to me, is the act of solving problems and developing solutions by instructing computers on what to do. Every problem is a puzzle that needs to be analyzed and solved. This process of solving complex problems and watching as a solution slowly begins to develop is an amazing feeling for me.

This is why I choose computer science.
